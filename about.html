About Me

Hi, I’m Emily Wong

I'm a senior at Northwestern University (class of 2021) studying economics and journalism. My passions include business, software and social activism-- both to write about and to pursue in my spare time.

Over the past few years, I've interned at several tech companies (see my LinkedIn), where I developed my software engineering and product management skills. In November, I finished an internship with The Mom Project, where I wrote articles and created social media content about how to succeed in your career as a mom. Currently, I'm a contributing writer at Candor, where I cover stories about the software and business industry, including internship programs in Big Tech, startups to watch, Q&As with professionals and more. I also write about more in-depth stories about current events in business on my Medium blog.

I believe that media has the power to democratize the world, and information is a key tool in leveling the playing field for all of us. Whether it's closing the wage gap by giving working moms advice on negotiating their salaries, or calling out a company's unethical practices, I'm excited to use journalism to make a difference.
